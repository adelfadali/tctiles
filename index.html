Mastering the Details: FAQ

At True Craft Tile and Stone, we believe that the beauty of a shower is secondary only to its integrity. Most standard "contractor-grade" showers rely on cement board and a plastic liner. While these materials are common, they are fundamentally flawed. Cement board is water-resistant, but it is not waterproof. Over time, moisture moves through the tile and grout, saturating the board and eventually reaching the wood studs behind it. This creates a hidden environment for mold and rot that may not be visible for years but will eventually compromise your home’s structure.

Our approach is fundamentally different because we are Schluter-Kerdi Authorized installers. We utilize a complete system-based approach where every component—the drain, the shower pan, and the walls—is integrated into a single, vapor-tight envelope. We start with Kerdi-Board, a lightweight, waterproof, and dimensionally stable substrate that replaces traditional heavy cement boards. Every seam and corner is then sealed with Kerdi-Band using specialized thin-set mortar. This ensures that water is managed on the surface of the system, never allowing it to penetrate into the wall cavity.

Beyond the waterproofing, our technical precision in the layout phase is what truly sets us apart. We don't just "start laying tile." we perform complex mathematical layouts to ensure that all cuts are balanced and symmetrical. We pay close attention to the "wrap" of the tile patterns in corners so that the design remains continuous. This level of planning prevents small, unsightly slivers of tile at the edges and creates a visual flow that mirrors high-end luxury resorts. When you invest in a shower from True Craft Tile and Stone, you are investing in 35 years of engineering expertise combined with artisanal finishing.

Fireplace tiling is one of the most technical areas of interior masonry because it involves extreme thermal expansion and contraction. At True Craft Tile and Stone, we understand that a fireplace isn't just a focal point; it's a heat-generating appliance that requires specific material choices and installation techniques to prevent cracking or tile failure. The cycle of heating and cooling creates immense stress on the bond between the tile and the substrate. If the wrong thin-set or improper expansion joints are used, the tiles will eventually pop or "tent" as the heat causes the materials to expand at different rates.

Our fireplace installation process begins with a thermal assessment of the surround. We use high-performance, polymer-modified mortars specifically designed for high-heat environments. These mortars maintain their flexibility under extreme temperature shifts, allowing the tile to expand without breaking the bond. For the substrate, we ensure that the surface is non-combustible and structurally reinforced to handle the weight of heavy stone or large-format tiles. We also pay strict attention to local Shakopee and Minneapolis building codes regarding clearances to combustible materials, ensuring your fireplace is as safe as it is beautiful.

Design-wise, a fireplace offers a unique opportunity for "book-matching" or complex stone layouts. Whether you are looking for a sleek, modern floor-to-ceiling porcelain slab or a traditional stacked-stone hearth, we prioritize the "architectural alignment" of the project. We ensure that the mantel, the firebox, and the floor transition are all perfectly integrated. Because the fireplace is often the center of the living room, we take extra time to hand-select stone pieces for color consistency and grain direction. A True Craft fireplace is designed to be a permanent, high-performance masterpiece that adds significant resale value to your home while serving as a safe, stunning gathering place for your family.

Commercial tile installation is a different beast entirely, focused heavily on liability, slip resistance, and extreme durability. In a residential setting, aesthetics often drive the decision-making process. In a commercial environment—such as a restaurant kitchen, a hotel lobby, or a retail store—performance and safety are the primary mandates. At True Craft Tile and Stone, we approach commercial projects with a focus on DCOF (Dynamic Coefficient of Friction) ratings. We ensure that all selected materials meet or exceed the ANSI A326.3 standard for slip resistance in wet environments, protecting business owners from potential liability issues.

The technical requirements for commercial substrates are also significantly higher. In a high-traffic lobby, the floor must withstand thousands of footfalls daily, as well as the weight of heavy equipment and cleaning machinery. We utilize specialized commercial-grade uncoupling membranes that protect the tile from the vibrations and shifting common in large commercial buildings. For industrial kitchens, we often recommend epoxy grouts. Unlike standard cementitious grout, epoxy is 100% non-porous and resistant to the acids, fats, and harsh industrial degreasers used in commercial cleaning. This prevents the growth of bacteria and ensures the facility passes health inspections with ease.

Finally, we understand that for commercial clients, "time is money." Commercial projects require strict adherence to timelines and coordination with other trades (plumbers, electricians, and inspectors). Our 35+ years of experience allows us to manage these complex logistical schedules without sacrificing quality. We provide comprehensive documentation, including technical data sheets (TDS) and safety data sheets (SDS) for all materials used, ensuring that your commercial facility is fully compliant with all Twin Cities building regulations. Whether it’s a 100-square-foot restroom or a 5,000-square-foot showroom, True Craft brings industrial-strength precision to every commercial square inch.
