The Science of a Waterproof Shower

In the humid climate of Minnesota, a shower is more than just a place to wash—it's a technical challenge. Many homeowners in Shakopee and Prior Lake deal with mold and mildew caused by outdated "cement board and plastic" methods. At True Craft Tile & Stone, we eliminate these risks entirely by utilizing the **Schluter-Kerdi System**.

This advanced waterproofing method creates a vapor-tight seal that ensures moisture stays in the drain and never enters your home's structural framing. Whether we are installing a standard alcove shower or a massive walk-in wet room, our commitment to technical perfection remains the same.

Designing Your Spa-Like Experience

Design is where your vision meets our expertise. We specialize in **Curbless Entry (Zero-Entry)** showers, which provide a modern, seamless look while ensuring accessibility for all ages. We take the time to plan every tile layout before the first bag of thinset is even opened. This "Dry-Fit" process ensures that we don't end up with small, ugly "sliver" cuts at the corners or the ceiling—a hallmark of amateur installation.

Material Quality & Durability

We don't cut corners on materials because we know that the price of failure in a shower is too high. We use premium modified thinsets from **Laticrete** and **Mapei** that allow for the slight expansion and contraction of tile as temperatures shift—essential for homes in the Twin Cities. For grout, we recommend **Ultracolor Plus FA**, which is drop-in-water resistant and never requires sealing, saving you hours of maintenance over the life of your bathroom.

Investment: What to Expect

A master-level custom shower in the Minneapolis area typically ranges from **$6,500 to $18,000**. This investment covers the complete demolition of the old unit, structural inspection of the subfloor, the full Schluter waterproofing system, and the meticulous installation of your selected tile. Factors that influence cost include:

  • Tile Type: Porcelain is cost-effective, while natural marble or intricate glass mosaics require more labor.
  • Geometry: Rectangular showers are standard; custom benches, angled walls, or vaulted ceilings add to the complexity.
  • Plumbing: Adding rain heads, body sprays, or hand-held units increases the technical difficulty of the tile cuts.
